Personas and Storyboarding

For this project I interviewed and observed users interacting with an interface, which I chose to be a DSLR camera, create personas based on interviwing them, and draw a storyboard based on their interaction with the interface.

DSLR Interface

Not a reader? Go to a more interactive version to learn about the camera!

  • Lens is the eye of the camera. You rotate the end of the lens to determine focus
  • Focus is the area in the photo you want to be captured with best clarity
  • Shutter Button is the button to click to capture the photo
  • Viewfinder is a small window to look through to compose photo
  • Shooting Mode corresponds to the mode selected in Mode Dial
  • Mode dials determine the mode. For simplicity let's stick to Manual and Automatic

Interviews and Observations

Statistics only give you "how" and "when"; users give you "why". I interviewed three different users about their lifestyle, expectations, motives and challenges about their camera, and observed how they interact with the interface.

    Lifestyle Question

  1. What do you enjoy? What do you do in your free time?
    • User 1 is a 20yr biotechnology student who loves to photograph nature. User 2 is a 40yr Noogler who is a casual photographer. User 3 is a 25yr old Architecture student who has a love hate relationship with the user.

    Expectation Questions

  2. Have you used/do you use a digital camera? What do you use the camera for?
    • User 1 has been obsessed with her camera since 8th grade and aims to take the perfect pictures. User 2 has been using a camera for a couple years after being inspired by a friend. It gives him a getaway from his “real life”. User 3 only uses his camera for course credit and is not a huge fan because of its size and weight.
  3. How would one (or you) define your relationship with your camera?
    • User 1 refers to her camera as her baby because of its monetary value and the fact that she can manipulate pictures to make it look perfect. User 2 finds his camera more reliable than a human being. User 3’ has a conditional and flaky relationship with his camera because it has given him both good and bad times.

    Experience Questions

  4. What was it like to use the camera ? How did it feel?
    • User 1 feels satisfied, happy and creative while using the camera and brags about taking the best pictures. User 2 finds time to just fly past by and also a little self conscious about his surroundings. User 3 initially found it hard to use it and loves capturing people’s, more importantly family’s, pictures and glancing back at them
  5. On a scale of 1 to 10, can you rate how successful you think you were?
    • User 1 is usually 10/10 but gets very disappointed if she isn’t. User 2 doesn’t really judge being successful because he’s doing it for his happiness. User 3 ranks it as 4/10 as he gave up on it quickly after learning the technical aspect.

    Motive Question

  6. Do you expect to gain anything from using a DSLR?
    • User 1 would like some popularity and perfection. User 2 just wants something to be there for him. User 3 just likes the emotional aspect of pictures.

    Challenges Question

  7. If you had a magic wand, would you change something about the camera?
    • Everything about the camera is perfect for User 1 as she has the best camera. User 2 would like to normalize automatic mode and simplify the process. User 3 would like to reduce its size, weight and sensitiveness.

A persona represents a type of a group of users of an interface. Empathy maps outline what this type of user says, does, thinks, and feels. Below are two empathy maps for two different personas for the DSLR camera based on the respective interviews and observations.
