Input/Output

Introduction

To design a user-driven website, designers are mostly in a situation to design a container for future inputs, rather than designing a layout that marries a specific form to content. In this project, I explore web as a platform for dynamic contents, focusing on how designing the container could influence the future contents.

Lofi Prototyping with Wireframes

I started off by brainstorming different input and outcomes through wireframes.

I was sitting on the rooftop at night and started wondering how the sky would look like if the earth spun faster or if we were closer. I wanted to have different inputs for bigger size and greater distance to see any planet by clicking on it.

Another idea was to show a time lapse video through sliding where you could choose a season and accordingly the scene would have images and sound of the same place over time in a day.

This idea was inspired by my five year old niece who is obsessed with slime and her pretend laptop. You can choose the color of the slime and drag it around with your mouse.

I was inspired by p5.js and wanted to utilize it to make spiral art digitally. The users would be able to input the number of lines they want, their opacity and width, and the rotation speed.

I am a huge potter head and was rewatching the scene where the Fat Lady sings and shatters the glass. I wanted to create an effect from speech to text which involved recognizing the emotion through the voice and animating text.

Initial Hifi Prototyping

After some feedback, I started contextualizing each input/output. I was struck by two ideas: the spiral art and the background time lapse. The spiral art has a huge significance in my religion and seemed like an interesting aspect to explore. Since I'm far away from home almost the entire year, I would often wonder how it would be if I was at home right now and browse through my pictures. I ended up making prototypes for each of them

Revised Hifi Prototyping

Since I had a more emotional connect to the time lapse prototype, I decided to go with it. I had the main theme of personalizing it more instead of zooming in on your city which felt like it was defeating the purpose.

Data Collection

I chose popular cities in US as home: New York, Boston, and Philly for the relative ease of high res images. I will be adding more cities as I go.

Since I'm not from any of these cities, I asked my friends from NYC, Boston and Philly to see what they considered home. I collected images and wrote captions based on this data.

Final Website

After one more iteration, I figured out that nostalgia was my theme and portrayed that on the landing page, instead of the night sky, and made pictures more immersive.

Conclusion

In this project, I learned a lot about how to convey my mood in the form of webpages. The dynamic content for different inputs truly shapes the experiences of users. To have an immersive experience, the dimensions of an object can play a huge role.
